Recent work improves Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) by guiding their intermediate representations, either with external pretrained encoders or with self-contained objectives over internal layers. While effective, most internal methods still treat hidden states as static objects to align or disperse. We revisit this view from the role of projection heads: rather than serving only as dimension adapters, a properly conditioned predictor may transfer the predictable component of a later representation without forcing two layers to collapse into the same feature space. Motivated by this perspective, we propose \emph{ReSync}, a lightweight self-distillation method that learns timestep-conditioned transitions across transformer depth. ReSync predicts later-layer hidden states from earlier-layer states at the same denoising timestep and regularizes these predictions with compositional consistency, so that long depth transitions agree with compositions of shorter ones. This same-timestep formulation keeps the main training cost close to internal layer-alignment baselines. We evaluate ReSync through generation quality, component ablations, transition prediction and compositional errors, predictor-capacity studies, and representation diagnostics. Cross-timestep prediction and skipped-depth inference are treated as exploratory extensions rather than primary claims.
