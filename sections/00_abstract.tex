Recent work has improved Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) by guiding their intermediate representations, either through external pretrained encoders or self-alignment between selected internal layers. Although effective, external guidance introduces additional model dependencies, while self-alignment methods often rely on heuristic choices of layers, noise levels, and projection heads, and still treat internal representations as static features to be matched. We revisit this perspective and propose \emph{ReSync}, a lightweight self-distillation method that regularizes Diffusion Transformers through internal representation dynamics rather than direct feature alignment. ReSync learns transitions from earlier hidden states to future ones, conditioned on layer and timestep information, and enforces compositional consistency so that long-range transitions agree with compositions of shorter transitions. This design allows different layers to preserve their own computational roles while sharing useful internal guidance. Experiments show that ReSync improves generation quality over direct-alignment baselines with minimal parameter overhead, without external encoders or additional data. These results suggest that modeling how representations evolve across depth and denoising time offers a more flexible alternative to matching selected hidden states.
