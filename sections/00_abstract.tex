Recent works improve Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) by guiding their intermediate representations, either with external pretrained encoders or with self-contained objectives over internal layers. While effective, most internal methods still treat hidden states as static objects to align or disperse. Projection heads used in representation alignment suggest that direct matching often benefits from a learned interface, but such heads are usually local to selected alignment pairs. Motivated by this perspective, we propose \emph{ReSyn}, a lightweight self-distillation method that turns internal guidance into timestep-conditioned transition prediction across transformer depth. ReSync predicts later-layer hidden states from earlier-layer states at the same denoising timestep and regularizes these predictions with compositional consistency, so that long depth transitions agree with compositions of shorter ones. This same-timestep formulation keeps the main training cost close to internal layer-alignment baselines. We evaluate ReSync through generation quality, component ablations, transition prediction and compositional errors, predictor-capacity studies, and representation diagnostics. Skipped-depth inference is treated as an exploratory extension rather than a primary claim.
