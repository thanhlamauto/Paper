Diffusion transformers benefit from internal representation alignment, which can provide useful guidance during training. Existing approaches either rely on external pretrained representations or align fixed internal layer pairs, making alignment heuristic and architecture-dependent. We propose \emph{ReSync}, a lightweight shared layer-aware transition predictor that learns cross-depth transitions between arbitrary internal layers. By modeling relations across depth, ReSync enables flexible internal alignment, reduces manual layer selection, and improves diffusion transformer representations with small training overhead and no standard-sampling cost. Experiments show improved generation quality, stronger intermediate representations, and ablations that validate the predictor design and pair-selection strategy.
