Representation Alignment has been shown to improve the the generatrive performance of Diffusion Transformer. Diffusion transformers benefit from internal representation alignment, which can provide useful guidance during training. Existing approaches either rely on external pretrained representation learning models or require manual selection of DiT layer pairs for alignment. We propose \emph{ReSync}, a lightweight shared layer-aware transition predictor that learns cross-depth transitions between arbitrary internal layers. By modeling relations across depth, ReSync enables flexible internal alignment, avoids manual layer selection, and improves diffusion transformer representations with small training overhead. Experiments show improved generation quality, stronger intermediate representations, and ablations that validate the predictor design and pair-selection strategy.
