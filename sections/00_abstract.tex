Recent work improves Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) by guiding their intermediate representations, either with external pretrained encoders or with self-contained objectives over internal layers. While effective, most internal methods still treat hidden states as static objects to align or disperse. Projection heads used in representation alignment suggest that useful internal supervision often requires a learned interface rather than direct matching. Motivated by this perspective, we propose \emph{ReSync}, a lightweight self-distillation method that uses timestep-conditioned transition prediction as an amortized self-alignment interface. ReSync predicts later-layer hidden states from earlier-layer states, allowing recoverable internal signals to supervise many source layers rather than a fixed alignment pair. A compositional consistency objective ties short and long transitions, and standard sampling discards the predictor. We evaluate ReSync through generation quality, component ablations, transition diagnostics, predictor-capacity studies, and representation analyses. Skipped-depth inference is treated as an exploratory extension rather than a primary claim.
