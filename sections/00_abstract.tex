Representation alignment has been shown to improve the generative performance of diffusion transformers. However, existing approaches either rely on external pretrained representation models or require manually selected internal layer pairs, tying the alignment signal to specific representation choices. We propose \emph{ReAlign}, a self-contained framework that models internal representation alignment as conditional transition prediction. Given an activation from one diffusion-transformer layer, ReAlign predicts the activation of another layer conditioned on the source layer, target layer, and denoising timestep. This allows a single lightweight neural predictor to support alignment across arbitrary layer pairs while capturing shared spatial structure and pair-specific representation differences. To stabilize long-range transitions, we further introduce a consistency objective that encourages direct transitions to agree with compositions of shorter transitions. Experiments on diffusion transformer training show that ReAlign improves generation quality over direct internal-alignment baselines with a small training-time overhead and without external encoders.
