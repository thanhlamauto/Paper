Representation alignment (RA) has recently emerged as an effective strategy for improving the generative capability of Diffusion Transformers (DiTs). However, existing methods typically rely on external pretrained representation models or require labor-intensive manual selection of layer pairs for alignment. In this work, we introduce \emph{LARA}, a novel RA framework that enables flexible alignment across arbitrary layer pairs within a DiT. Our approach is motivated by the observation that intermediate DiT activations exhibit both layer-specific semantic variations and shared structural regularities. Building on this insight, LARA learns a lightweight, layer-aware transition predictor that captures common structural information in its shared parameters while adapting to varying semantic gaps of different layer pairs through conditional layer embeddings. This design enables scalable and unified representation alignment throughout the network without external supervision or handcrafted layer selection. Extensive experiments and ablation studies demonstrate that LARA consistently improves generative quality and yields stronger representations for downstream tasks.
