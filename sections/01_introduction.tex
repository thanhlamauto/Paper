\section{Introduction}

Diffusion and flow-matching models are now a standard route to high-fidelity generation~\citep{ho2020ddpm,song2019score,song2021sde,lipman2022flow}. Transformer backbones such as DiT and SiT make these models scalable, but training remains expensive~\citep{peebles2023scalable,ma2024sit}. A useful way to improve training is to guide the hidden representations learned across transformer depth.

Existing guidance is either external or internal. External methods align diffusion-transformer states with pretrained visual representations such as DINOv2 or vision-language features~\citep{yu2024repa,wang2025haste,leng2025repae,wang2026vaerepa,oquab2023dinov2,wang2024qwen2vl}. They provide strong targets, but add teacher computation and tie the recipe to a particular encoder. Internal methods avoid this dependency by using the diffusion transformer's own layer hierarchy~\citep{jiang2025sra,haghighi2026layersync}.

The key obstacle is that each depth has its own representation coordinates. Representation quality varies across depth and often peaks before the final decoder-like blocks~\citep{mukhopadhyay2024,kim2025,stracke2025,haghighi2026layersync}. Direct internal alignment must therefore choose which weak and strong layers to match. This choice is heuristic, architecture-dependent, and sensitive to model depth; it also asks raw hidden states from different depths to meet despite representation mismatch.

We propose \emph{ReAlign}, a lightweight layer-aware transition predictor for flexible cross-depth internal alignment. Instead of aligning one manually selected layer pair, ReAlign samples source--target layers and learns a shared predictor that maps an earlier hidden state into a later layer's representation space. Conditioning on the source layer, target layer, and denoising timestep lets the same module support arbitrary layer pairs without a separate adapter per pair.

The idea is simple. ReAlign learns a bridge between layer distributions, so supervision follows the transition across depth instead of a few fixed endpoints. Uniform layer-pair sampling already provides a strong self-aligned signal; a pair-selection strategy then biases training toward transitions that are informative and learnable. This keeps internal alignment self-contained, less heuristic, and lightweight.

Empirically, ReAlign improves generation under matched training settings; on ImageNet $256\times256$ with SiT-B/2 and no classifier-free guidance, it improves FID from $25.78$ for the baseline and $21.32$ for LayerSync to $18.88$. Scaling results, objective ablations, pair-sampling diagnostics, and representation probes support the same message: a shared layer-aware transition predictor makes internal alignment more flexible while improving diffusion transformer representations.

Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=0.25em,itemsep=0.15em,parsep=0pt]
    \item We introduce a lightweight layer-aware transition predictor for flexible cross-depth internal alignment in diffusion transformers.
    \item We use stochastic layer-pair training and a simple pair-selection strategy to reduce reliance on hand-crafted layer choices.
    \item We provide generation, scaling, ablation, representation, and overhead analyses showing that ReAlign improves sample quality and internal representations with minimal standard-sampling cost.
\end{itemize}
