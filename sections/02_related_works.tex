\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Diffusion transformers.}
Diffusion and flow-based objectives provide a continuous path between data and noise~\citep{ho2020ddpm,song2019score,song2021sde,lipman2022flow}. DiT and SiT replace U-Net backbones with scalable transformers, making depth-wise hidden representations a natural object of study~\citep{peebles2023scalable,ma2024sit}. Recent analyses show that these intermediate representations are structured and that their quality is tied to generative performance~\citep{mukhopadhyay2024,kim2025,stracke2025}.

\paragraph{Representation alignment for diffusion models.}
REPA improves DiT and SiT training by aligning hidden states to external visual features~\citep{yu2024repa}. Follow-up methods strengthen this recipe through end-to-end variants, different teachers, or spatially faithful targets~\citep{wang2025haste,leng2025repae,wang2026vaerepa,singh2025irepa}. These methods show the value of representation guidance, while also depending on external encoders such as DINOv2~\citep{oquab2023dinov2}.

\paragraph{Internal layer alignment and self-alignment.}
Self-contained methods derive guidance from the diffusion model itself. SRA uses an internal EMA teacher~\citep{jiang2025sra,tarvainen2017meanteacher}; Dispersive Loss and related diversity regularizers shape the spread of internal features~\citep{wang2025dispersive,yang2026diversedit}; LayerSync aligns selected weaker layers with stronger internal layers~\citep{haghighi2026layersync}. These approaches avoid external teachers, but still depend on direct matching or chosen hidden states. ReSync keeps the signal internal and replaces fixed raw-state matching with a shared layer-aware transition predictor over many source--target pairs.
