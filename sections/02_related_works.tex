\section{Related Work}

\noindent\textbf{Representation guidance for diffusion transformers.} Diffusion models can be viewed as multi-level denoising objectives, a perspective connected to denoising autoencoders and later analyses of diffusion features as self-supervised representations~\citep{vincent2008extracting,xiang2023denoising}. Recent work shows that diffusion and flow-based generative models learn intermediate representations whose quality is closely tied to generative performance~\citep{mukhopadhyay2024, kim2025, stracke2025, yu2024repa}. REPA improves DiT and SiT training by aligning hidden states with external visual representations from pretrained encoders~\citep{yu2024repa}, and follow-up methods refine this paradigm through stronger training recipes, end-to-end variants, or more spatially faithful targets~\citep{wang2025haste,leng2025repae,wang2026vaerepa,singh2025irepa}. In particular, iREPA suggests that preserving spatial structure can be important beyond global semantic alignment, and reports improvements to REPA-style training by modifying the projection and normalization used for alignment~\citep{singh2025irepa}. These methods are effective, but their dependence on external teachers introduces additional computation, architectural coupling, and limited portability to domains where strong pretrained encoders are unavailable~\citep{oquab2023dinov2,yu2024repa}.

\noindent\textbf{Self-contained internal regularization.} Several recent methods avoid external encoders by deriving guidance from the diffusion model itself. Dispersive Loss encourages internal features to spread out in representation space, and related diversity-oriented regularizers aim to preserve useful variation across blocks~\citep{wang2025dispersive,yang2026diversedit}. SRA aligns earlier or noisier student features with later or cleaner features from an EMA teacher built from the same diffusion model~\citep{jiang2025sra,tarvainen2017meanteacher}. LayerSync aligns selected weaker layers with stronger internal layers, using the model's own hierarchy as intrinsic guidance~\citep{haghighi2026layersync}. Self-transcendence-style studies ask a related question of whether external feature guidance is indispensable for accelerating diffusion-transformer training~\citep{sun2026selftranscendence}. These methods show that self-contained signals can improve diffusion training, but they still rely on direct matching or diversity regularization over chosen hidden states. ReSync is complementary: it keeps the guidance self-contained while replacing fixed raw-state matching with one shared layer-aware predictor that can coordinate supervision across many source--target pairs.
