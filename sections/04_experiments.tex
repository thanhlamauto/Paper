\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

This section tests the claims made in the introduction. If useful internal guidance is better exposed through a learned layer-aware interface, then ReSync should improve generation, its component ablations should identify the useful parts of the objective, and representation probes should show stronger intermediate features. We organize the experiments around three questions:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Does ReSync improve generation quality and training efficiency across domains and model sizes? (\Cref{sec:image_generation,sec:human_motion_generation})
    \item Which parts of the predictor and layer-pair sampling strategy matter? (\Cref{sec:ablation_study})
    \item Does ReSync improve the backbone representations it supervises? (\Cref{sec:representation_analysis})
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Image Generation}
\label{sec:image_generation}
\label{sec:experimental_setup}

\paragraph{Implementation details.}
Unless otherwise stated, we follow the class-conditional ImageNet training setup of SiT~\citep{ma2024sit}. We train on ImageNet~\citep{deng2009imagenet} at $256\times256$ resolution using the preprocessing protocol of ADM~\citep{dhariwal2021diffusion}. Each image is encoded by the Stable Diffusion VAE from LDM~\citep{rombach2022ldm} into a latent tensor $z\in\mathbb{R}^{32\times32\times4}$. We evaluate the \texttt{B/2}, \texttt{L/2}, and \texttt{XL/2} SiT configurations with patch size $2$. ReSync trains the transition predictor jointly with the backbone using \Cref{eq:total_loss}. Unless specified, we use AdamW with learning rate $10^{-4}$ and batch size $256$. Additional details on architecture, transition-pair sampling, EMA decay, loss weights, training length, hardware, and compute budget are organized in \Cref{app:experimental_details}.

\paragraph{Evaluation.}
We report Frechet Inception Distance (FID)~\citep{heusel2017gans}, Inception Score (IS)~\citep{salimans2016improved}, precision, and recall~\citep{kynkaanniemi2019precision} using $50\mathrm{K}$ generated samples. We use the same reference statistics and evaluation implementation for all ReSync variants and baselines. Definitions for the diagnostic metrics that are not standard generation benchmarks are provided in \Cref{app:metric_definitions}.

\paragraph{Comparison methods.}
Our main comparisons are against the base SiT/DiT backbone and recent representation-regularized training methods. For self-contained guidance, we compare with Dispersive Loss~\citep{wang2025dispersive}, SRA~\citep{jiang2025sra}, and LayerSync~\citep{haghighi2026layersync}. For externally guided training, we compare with REPA~\citep{yu2024repa} and REED~\citep{wang2025flexible}. For completeness, we also report representative image-generation baselines: pixel-space diffusion models including ADM~\citep{dhariwal2021diffusion}, VDM++~\citep{kingma2023understanding}, Simple Diffusion~\citep{hoogeboom2023simple}, and CDM~\citep{ho2022cascaded}; latent diffusion and transformer-based models including LDM~\citep{rombach2022ldm}, U-ViT-H/2~\citep{bao2023all}, DiffiT~\citep{hatamizadeh2024diffit}, MDTv2-XL/2~\citep{gao2023mdtv2}, MaskDiT~\citep{zheng2023fast}, SD-DiT~\citep{zhu2024sddit}, DiT~\citep{peebles2023scalable}, and SiT~\citep{ma2024sit}; and autoregressive or predictive-generation baselines such as VAR~\citep{tian2024visual} and D-JEPA~\citep{chen2024jepa}. Detailed baseline descriptions and the parameter-matching protocol are deferred to \Cref{app:baseline_descriptions,app:baseline_protocol}.

\subsection{Text-Conditional Human Motion Generation}
\label{sec:human_motion_generation}

To test whether ReSync transfers beyond image generation, we additionally evaluate text-conditioned human motion generation on HumanML3D~\citep{guo2022generating}. Given a text description, the model generates a sequence of human poses represented as 3D joint trajectories.

\paragraph{Implementation details.}
We follow the MDM setup~\citep{tevet2022mdm} and use a transformer backbone with the same training budget for the baseline and ReSync variant. The main comparison trains MDM with and without ReSync for $600\mathrm{K}$ iterations; architecture and loss settings are reported in \Cref{app:resync_hyperparameters}.

\paragraph{Evaluation metrics.}
We report FID and R-Precision (top 3) using the standard HumanML3D evaluation protocol. Definitions and reporting conventions are provided in \Cref{app:metric_definitions}. Lower FID is better, while higher R-Precision indicates better text-motion retrieval alignment.

\paragraph{Results.}
\Cref{tab:humanml3d_results} reports the HumanML3D comparison. This experiment tests whether the same signal-extracting transition objective transfers to a smaller, non-image transformer without changing the generative task.

We also compare layer-pair sampling strategies to separate the main benefit of the shared predictor from the additional gain of choosing better transition pairs. \label{sec:representation_analysis}
