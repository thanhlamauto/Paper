\section{Limitations}

Our evaluation focuses on training-time internal alignment for diffusion transformers under standard sampling. Large-scale sweeps are expensive, so component and sampling ablations are run on the smaller backbone, while the main comparisons test whether the same recipe transfers across larger settings. ReSync does not claim to replace external teachers in every regime or to accelerate sampling; the predictor is removed for ordinary inference.

\section{Conclusion}

We introduced ReSync, a self-contained training objective for layer-aware self-alignment in Diffusion Transformers. ReSync replaces fixed raw-state matching with a shared predictor that maps earlier activations into later-layer coordinates across sampled layer pairs. This simple interface reduces reliance on hand-picked alignments, improves generation under matched settings, and produces stronger intermediate representations without changing the standard sampler.
