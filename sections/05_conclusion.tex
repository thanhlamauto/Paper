\section{Conclusion and Discussion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced LARA, a self-contained framework for layer-aware representation alignment in Diffusion Transformers. Instead of directly matching a manually selected pair of hidden states, LARA learns a shared transition predictor that maps activations across arbitrary layer pairs while adapting to source and target depth. This turns internal supervision into a reusable alignment signal and improves both generation quality and intermediate representation quality with small training overhead.

Our study focuses on training-time alignment under standard sampling. Component and sampling ablations are run on the smaller backbone, while the final recipe is validated on larger settings to check transfer across depth and scale. The transition interface also suggests a broader use of internal states as controllable computation points, including lightweight distillation or inference-time interfaces, which we leave for future work.

\paragraph{Broader impact.}
LARA aims to improve the training efficiency and representation quality of generative transformers, which can reduce the cost of model development. As with other image and motion generation methods, stronger generators may also be misused for synthetic media creation. We do not release a new dataset or pretrained model in this submission; any future release should follow the licenses of the underlying assets and include appropriate usage guidance.
